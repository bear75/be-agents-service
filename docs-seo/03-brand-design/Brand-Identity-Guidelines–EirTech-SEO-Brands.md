# Brand Identity Guidelines – EirTech SEO Product Brands

This document defines the brand identity for three new EirTech home care SEO brands: **Hemtjänstguide.se**, **Sverigeshemtjänst.se**, and **Nackahemtjänst.se**. Each brand has its own unique identity but remains aligned with the EirTech group's design language. Use this guide as a reference for designers, marketers, and AI assistants to ensure consistency across visual design and tone of voice.

## Color System

### Shared Group Colors

All brands use EirTech's core palette to reinforce group affiliation. The two primary group colors are:

- **Deep Slate** – `#1C2E35` (dark slate blue). Used as a primary neutral: background, headlines, and text. Conveys a sense of depth, professionalism, and authority across all products.
- **Eir Aqua** – `#3FD2C7` (vibrant aqua). Used as a signature accent across the group: for secondary buttons, links, hover states, or subtle highlights. This aqua injects energy and innovation, tying each product to the EirTech brand.

### Product Accent Colors

Each product brand adds one unique accent color to this base palette. These accents differentiate the brands while complementing the group colors. They should be used for primary call-to-action elements, highlights, and graphic motifs specific to that product (e.g. in the logo, icons, or emphasis backgrounds):

- **Hemtjänstguide.se – Amber Accent** (`#F59E0B`): A warm amber-orange that feels friendly and guiding. This inviting hue suggests optimism and support, ideal for a guide service. Use it for primary buttons, icons, or important UI highlights on Hemtjänstguide.se. The amber accent paired with Eir Aqua and Deep Slate balances warmth with the high-tech feel of the group palette, signaling an approachable yet credible resource.

- **Sverigeshemtjänst.se – Blue Accent** (`#0EA5E9`): A bold "Swedish" blue that conveys trust, reliability, and nationwide scope. This accent (inspired by the Swedish national palette) underscores authority and comprehensive coverage of Sweden's home care. Use it for primary actions, headers, or graphic elements on Sverigeshemtjänst.se to give an inclusive, trustworthy feel. The blue accent harmonizes with the aqua and slate base, reinforcing an image of credibility and broad reach.

- **Nackahemtjänst.se – Rose Accent** (`#F43F5E`): A vibrant rose pink-red that embodies care and energy. This unique accent gives Nackahemtjänst.se a personable, caring character reflecting a local community vibe. Use the rose color for Nacka-specific call-to-action buttons, highlights, and icons. It adds a touch of warmth and urgency (attracting attention to local options) while still aligning with the shared aqua/slate foundation.

### Usage Tip

In each brand's UI, the accent color should be the dominant highlight (for example, the color of primary buttons, active menu items, or logo icon), whereas Eir Aqua can serve as a secondary highlight (like link hovers, secondary buttons, or subtle illustrations). Deep Slate is generally used for text, footer backgrounds, or any dark contrast needs. By keeping Deep Slate and Eir Aqua consistent, the brands feel related; by using the designated accent boldly (but judiciously), each product's interface becomes immediately recognizable.

## Logo Direction

All three logos should visually indicate they belong to the EirTech family while maintaining distinction through color. Follow a unified style: clean, modern, and symbolic, echoing EirTech's design motifs (notably the "map dot" and flow elements).

### Icon Motif

Design each logo around a similar core symbol that suggests location and connectivity (as hinted by a map pin/dot and flowing lines motif). For example, a stylized map marker or dot connected by a curved path/flourish can be the base. This shape should be consistent across the three logos – think of it as a common "DNA" linking them. The icon's style: geometric with soft curves (no sharp angles), conveying a sense of flow and guidance. The flowing line element can symbolize the stream of care or guidance, while the dot/pin represents a focal point (like a location or person).

### Family Resemblance

Ensure the icons are in the same illustrative style (same line thickness or filled style, same level of simplicity) so that side by side, it's clear they are part of one suite. The icon should subtly remind viewers of the broader EirTech aesthetic (for instance, if EirTech or Caire logos use certain circular or flowing shapes, incorporate a nod to that). Affiliation can also be signaled by incorporating the group color: for instance, a small element or highlight in the icon could be Eir Aqua or Deep Slate, while the main body is the product's accent color.

### Color Differentiation

Each logo icon primarily uses the product's unique accent color as identified above. This instantly distinguishes Hemtjänstguide (amber icon), Sverigeshemtjänst (blue icon), and Nackahemtjänst (rose icon) from each other. The logo text (the name of the site) can be set in Deep Slate or a neutral dark color for consistency and legibility. Avoid multicolor complexity; stick to one accent + possibly a small Aqua or white element if needed for contrast or the "flow" design.

### Composition

The logo consists of icon + wordmark (the site name). Do not include any tagline or slogan in the lockup – just the icon and the product name for a clean look. The icon can be placed to the left of the text or integrated (for example, preceding a capital letter or as a dot in a letter if it fits elegantly). Maintain clear space around the logo – at least the height of the icon on all sides – to ensure it isn't crowded by other elements.

### Style & Scalability

Use vector shapes with flat colors (no elaborate gradients or shadows in the primary logo) for a modern, flat design style. The icon should be recognizable at small sizes (e.g., favicons or social avatars), which means keeping it simple and avoiding tiny details. All logos should work on both light and dark backgrounds (prepare a reversed version if necessary: e.g., icon in white or accent color on a dark background). Because the icon designs have similar forms, people will perceive a cohesive brand system, and the different accent colors will allow quick identification of each specific site.

### Logo Inspiration

Imagine an abstract map pin combined with a gentle swoosh or circular arrow wrapping around it (implying guidance or flow). All three logos could use this concept: e.g., a solid circle or pin (perhaps in Eir Aqua or white) encircled or connected by a curved line in the product's accent color. This way, the logos echo movement and guidance (the "flow"), while the dot can hint at location or individual care points. Keep the overall vibe friendly and innovative, matching EirTech's tech-forward yet human-centric ethos.

## Typography Guidelines

A consistent typographic style across the brands ensures they feel related. All brands should use a clean, sans-serif typeface for a modern and approachable appearance. We recommend using **Inter** (a highly legible, modern geometric sans-serif) as the primary font across all three brands, for both web and print materials. (Alternatively, **Work Sans** can be used if needed – it has a similar clean grotesk aesthetic. The key is to pick one and use it consistently across these products to maintain unity.)

### Basic Typeface Settings

- **Font Family**: Inter (or Work Sans) – use the same font family for all to unify the look. This font is chosen for its clarity at small sizes and contemporary feel (aligned with tech branding yet friendly in tone).
- **Typography Tone**: Generally use sentence case (rather than all-caps) for a more approachable, human voice, unless a specific UI element calls for uppercase. Maintain ample line spacing and avoid overly condensed text to keep readability high, especially for an audience that might include seniors or caregivers.

### Weight & Style by Brand

To subtly reinforce each brand's personality, we vary the font weights emphasis per brand, inspired by EirTech's existing hierarchy (EirTech corporate uses bolder weights for authority; Caire uses regular weights for friendliness; ScheduleWell strikes a medium balance for clarity). All brands will use Inter/Work Sans in a range of weights, but the primary weight emphasis differs:

#### Hemtjänstguide.se – Approachable & Helpful

Use Regular to Medium weight for most interface text. For example, headings and menu items can be Inter Medium (500), and body text Inter Regular (400). This slightly lighter typographic voice feels friendly, informative, and not overbearing – perfect for a guide meant to help users. Avoid extremely bold text so that the tone remains warm and guiding rather than authoritative. Italics or a lighter weight can be used sparingly for empathetic quotes or tips, reinforcing a caring tone.

#### Sverigeshemtjänst.se – Authoritative & Trustworthy

Lean on SemiBold to Bold weights to convey credibility and importance. Major headings could be Inter Bold (700) for a strong presence, with subheadings in Inter SemiBold (600). Body text can be a clear Regular (400) or Medium (500) to remain legible. The overall impression should be slightly more weighty and formal, signaling that this is a definitive, trustworthy source ("Sweden's Home Care" implies an official or comprehensive resource). The heavier font weights give a sense of authority and stability, similar to how EirTech's own brand asserts expertise.

#### Nackahemtjänst.se – Clear & Focused

Aim for a middle ground using Medium and SemiBold weights (mirroring the ScheduleWell brand's focus on clarity). For example, section titles and important labels can be Inter SemiBold (600), while most text (including paragraph content and smaller headings) is Inter Medium (500). This approach yields a clean, easy-to-read interface where information is conveyed with straightforward clarity. The tone is neither as heavy as Sverigeshemtjänst nor as light as Hemtjänstguide – reflecting a pragmatic, no-nonsense local guide that still feels friendly. Use bold only for occasional emphasis or critical labels so that the hierarchy remains clear without shouting at the user.

### Additional Typography Tips

Keep typography consistent in scale across the brands for a unified feel. For instance, establish a base font size (e.g., 16px for body text) and a modular heading scale (H1 through H5) that can be applied to all three sites with only the weight variations as noted. Use ample whitespace between lines and paragraphs to align with the airy layout style (see Visual System). Also, ensure high contrast between text and background (Deep Slate or black text on white, or white text on Deep Slate backgrounds) for accessibility. The chosen fonts (Inter/Work Sans) are optimized for screen reading, which is ideal since these brands mainly live as web platforms.

## Visual System & UI Guidelines

Consistency in layouts, shapes, and imagery across these products will make them all feel like part of the EirTech ecosystem. Below we outline guidance on layout/spacing, stylistic elements (flowing shapes and soft geometry), and UI usage of colors to create a coherent visual experience.

### Layout & Spacing

#### Clean, Open Layouts

Design pages with lots of white space (or very light neutral backgrounds) to avoid overwhelming users. Follow a simple grid (e.g., a 12-column responsive grid) with predictable margins and gutters. Each section on a page should have generous padding so content is well-separated – this not only improves readability (especially for older users or busy professionals) but also conveys a sense of calm and order.

#### Spacing Scale

Use a consistent spacing system (for example, an 8px or 4px baseline grid for margins, padding, and line heights). This means buttons, cards, and sections should have padding/margins in increments of that base (8, 16, 24px, etc.). Such consistency across the three brands makes the interfaces feel related and professionally designed. It also helps any shared components (like forms or tables) look and behave similarly on each site.

#### Responsive and Accessible Design

Ensure the design is mobile-friendly with layouts that can stack or reflow gracefully. Keep touch targets (buttons, links) sufficiently large (at least ~44px high) and spaced so that they're easy to tap – an aspect of layout that signals a user-centric approach. Also, maintain consistent placement for common elements (e.g., navigation menus, footer structure) across the sites; this fosters familiarity, so if someone knows one site, the others feel intuitive.

### Shapes, Imagery & Graphic Style (Flow & Soft Geometry)

#### Flowing Lines & Curves

Embrace the "flow" motif in backgrounds or illustrations. This could mean subtle curved lines or wave patterns in hero sections, or abstract graphics with swooshes and circular nodes (echoing the logo icon shape) to represent connectivity and movement. Avoid sharp, rigid graphics; instead opt for soft, rounded shapes that feel welcoming. For example, instead of a sharp zigzag separator between sections, use a gentle wavy line or a curve. This visual language of curves and flows suggests ease of use and a guiding path, reinforcing the idea that these platforms smoothly guide the user through information.

#### Soft Geometry

Use elements like rounded rectangles, circles, and organic blobs in the UI and marketing materials. Buttons and cards should have slightly rounded corners (e.g., a border-radius of 6–8px or more) rather than perfect right angles – this subtle softness makes the interface feel friendly and modern. Icons or illustrations should use simple geometric forms (circles for people or map pins, rounded corner squares for cards, etc.) and avoid overly intricate detail, aligning with a minimalistic, tech-savvy aesthetic.

#### Imagery and Photos

If using photographs (for example, on marketing pages or blog posts), choose images that evoke warm, positive emotions – caring caregivers, seniors being helped, community scenes, etc., with a light, airy composition. Apply a slight overlay tint in Eir Aqua or the site's accent color to unify photos with the brand palette if needed (e.g., a translucent aqua gradient overlay can give images an EirTech-family feel). Any decorative graphics (like illustrations or icon sets) should share a common style: flat design, line icons with uniform stroke width, or simple filled icons using the brand colors. This ensures that whether it's an icon on Hemtjänstguide or on Nackahemtjänst, it feels like part of the same icon family.

#### Motion & Interaction

If adding interactive effects, favor smooth transitions and gentle animations that follow the flow theme. For instance, hover effects on buttons can be a subtle color fade or a slight slide of an underline on links (avoiding jarring jumps). Any loading indicators or graphical elements (maybe a spinner or progress bar) could incorporate a circular motion or flowing dots – reinforcing the sense of continuity and modernity.

### UI Elements & Color Usage

#### Primary vs Secondary Actions

Establish a clear visual hierarchy for interactive elements using color and weight. The primary actions (e.g., main "Submit" or "Contact" buttons) on each site should be in the brand's accent color – this makes them stand out immediately to the user. For example, on Hemtjänstguide.se the primary buttons would be Amber; on Sverigeshemtjänst.se they'd be Blue; on Nackahemtjänst.se they'd be Rose. These buttons can have solid fills of the accent color with white text for strong contrast. Secondary buttons (less critical actions) or links can use Eir Aqua or a lighter tint of the accent as a border or text color, to denote a softer priority while still keeping within palette.

#### State Colors and Feedback

All three brands can share a common approach to success/warning/error colors, ideally aligned with the group style (for instance, green for success, yellow/orange for warnings, red for errors – possibly leveraging or slightly tweaking the accent colors if appropriate). However, ensure these standard UI feedback colors don't clash with the accent palette. Given the accents chosen: Amber and Rose are already close to "warning" and "error" tones respectively, so use them carefully (you may want a darker variant of rose for error messages on Nackahemtjänst, for example, to differentiate from normal accent usage). Consistency here helps users trust the feedback – e.g., a form error highlighted in red (rose variant) will be understood similarly across sites.

#### Typography in UI

As noted, use Deep Slate (or black/charcoal) for most text to ensure readability. On colored backgrounds or accent-colored buttons, use white text for contrast (e.g., white labels on an Amber button). The Inter/Work Sans font is highly readable; maintain sufficient font size (at least 16px for body, larger for headings) and contrast (check that the contrast meets accessibility standards, especially for the older audience on these sites). For instance, the Rose accent is quite vivid – white text on it is great, but if you ever put Deep Slate text on a rose background, ensure the rose is very light or the text is bold enough to read.

#### Icons and Illustrations in UI

When using icons alongside text (like feature lists or contact info), consider coloring the icons in the accent color to draw the eye and add visual interest, while text remains in neutral dark. Alternatively, a two-tone approach can be nice: part of an icon in the accent color and part in Aqua or Slate, to subtly include the group identity. Keep iconography consistent in style and proportion across the brands (you might even use the same icon set but just recolored per brand).

#### Component Styling

Form fields, cards, tables, etc., should follow a uniform style across these brands. For example, cards or info boxes might have a white background with a small colored edge or icon in the accent color. Hover or selection highlights in lists or menus could use a pale tint of the accent (e.g., Amber 100 for Hemtjänstguide) to indicate active states without overwhelming. All these small details, if kept consistent, mean a user who visits multiple of your sites feels a continuity of experience (which builds trust in the overall EirTech network).

In summary, the visual system should marry professionalism with warmth: a structured, airy layout (professional) combined with flowing shapes and friendly colors (warmth). This duality – tech credibility with a caring touch – is at the heart of EirTech's design philosophy and should shine through in each of the three product brands.

## Tone of Voice & Emotional Feel

Each brand serves a slightly different audience and purpose, so their tone of voice — the personality in text and communications — should be tailored accordingly. However, all three share EirTech's core values of innovation in care and should feel like part of one family (e.g., all should sound clear, genuine, and helpful). Below we describe each brand's tone and emotional resonance.

### Hemtjänstguide.se – Friendly, Supportive, Guiding

The tone of Hemtjänstguide.se is that of a helpful guide or advisor for individuals and families navigating home care. It should feel approachable, warm, and empowering. Write in a conversational and encouraging style – as if a knowledgeable friend is walking the reader through their options in simple terms. Emotionally, it should convey reassurance and optimism: readers should feel that "help is available and you can make informed decisions with confidence." Use positive language, focusing on possibilities and solutions (e.g., "You can find the right home care provider for your loved one" rather than "It's hard to find..."). Avoid jargon; use clear everyday language and explain any necessary terms since the audience may not be experts. The brand voice is caring and patient: it acknowledges the challenges users face (like caring for an elderly parent) and offers guidance with empathy. This builds trust and relatability, positioning Hemtjänstguide.se as a compassionate companion in the decision-making process.

### Sverigeshemtjänst.se – Expert, Comprehensive, Trustworthy

Sverigeshemtjänst.se should sound like the authoritative voice of Sweden's home care sector – professional, informative, yet still accessible. The tone is knowledgeable and reliable, instilling confidence that this source has the "big picture" view. Writing style can be a bit more formal and structured, reflecting the breadth of information (covering all of Sweden). However, avoid being overly stiff; it's a friendly authority, not a bureaucratic one. Think of an editorial tone: balanced, factual, and inclusive. Use inclusive language that speaks to a wide audience (since "Sweden's Home Care" implies serving all regions and stakeholders). The emotional feel is reassuring and empowering through knowledge. Readers (perhaps seniors or family decision-makers) should feel "informed and in good hands" after reading. This site might present data or comparisons (e.g., stats about home care in different municipalities) – do so in a clear, unbiased way. Trustworthiness is key: use consistent terminology, cite credible sources or regulations when relevant, and maintain a helpful yet objective tone. In summary, Sverigeshemtjänst.se is the trusted guidebook or encyclopedia: comprehensive but never overwhelming, authoritative but caring.

### Nackahemtjänst.se – Local, Caring, Community-Oriented

Nackahemtjänst.se speaks to the local community (Nacka municipality) with a tone that is personal, engaging, and action-oriented. It should feel neighborly and encouraging – like a local expert who truly cares about the community's well-being. The language can be slightly more informal or upbeat, given the local context (for instance, using "we" and "our community" to foster a sense of togetherness: "In Nacka, we pride ourselves on…"). The emotional undertone is empowerment and care: you want users to feel "our community cares about quality home care, and together we can ensure the best for our seniors." Stories or examples can be used to connect emotionally (e.g., highlighting a positive story of a Nacka resident finding great care). The voice should still remain informative and clear, but with a bit more warmth and motivational energy. Encourage local action: phrases like "discover options in Nacka" or "here's how Nacka leads the way…" involve readers directly. Because it's part of the EirTech family, it still maintains professionalism (well-structured content, correct info), but it's the most intimate and vibrant of the three voices. This balance makes Nackahemtjänst.se feel like a supportive local initiative backed by a strong platform.
