📖 Metrics Definitions

Continuity

Number of distinct caregivers (vehicles) assigned to each client over the scheduling period. Lower is better for quality of care.

Target: ≤ 15 caregivers per client

Excellent: ≤5 | Good: 6-10 | Acceptable: 11-15 | Poor: >15

CCI (Continuity of Care Index)

Measures consistency of caregiver-client relationships. Calculated as the probability that two consecutive visits are by the same caregiver.

CCI = (nc - 1) / (nv - 1)

where nc = visits by most frequent caregiver, nv = total visits

Range: 0.0 (no continuity) to 1.0 (perfect continuity)

Field Efficiency

Percentage of field time spent delivering care vs traveling between clients. Key performance indicator for operational efficiency.

Visit Time / (Visit Time + Travel Time)

Target: >67.5% (Slingor benchmark)

Excellent: >75% | Good: 68-75% | Poor: <68%

Efficiency (Excl Idle)

Overall staffing efficiency excluding empty shifts and idle time. Shows productive time as percentage of paid time.

Visit Time / (Shift Time - Idle Time)

Higher values indicate better capacity utilization.

Empty Shifts

Shifts with no assigned visits. Represents wasted capacity and labor cost with no value delivery.

Target: 0 empty shifts (0%)

Solution: Use from-patch to trim empty shifts after initial solve.

Unassigned Visits

Care visits that could not be assigned to any caregiver shift. Indicates capacity shortfall or constraint conflicts.

Target: <5% unassigned

Causes: Insufficient capacity, time window conflicts, geography constraints, or continuity requirements.

Visit Groups

Multiple visits that must be performed together (e.g., two-person care). Each group counts as one scheduling unit.

Example: 152 visit groups with 2 visits each = 304 total visits in groups.

Kolada Continuity

Swedish national quality indicator for home care continuity. Measures percentage of clients receiving care from ≤15 different personnel.

Clients with ≤15 caregivers / Total clients

National target: >85% of clients
