> **Purpose:** Map CAIRE product features to the ESS + FSR dual-model architecture. For each feature we state how we use Timefold ESS and/or FSR to deliver it.

## Overview

CAIRE’s scheduling product is built on two Timefold models:

- **ESS (Employee Shift Scheduling)** — who works when: shift creation/assignment, contracts, labor law, cost, continuity at the shift level.
- **FSR (Field Service Routing)** — who visits whom and in what order: route optimization, travel, visit assignment, continuity at the visit level.

The table below is the **feature → ESS/FSR roadmap**. Each row is a CAIRE feature; the columns describe how we achieve it with ESS, FSR, or both, and where the Timefold roadmap helps.

## Feature → ESS/FSR Mapping

### Pre-planning and “schedule all visits”

| CAIRE feature                                                     | ESS role                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        | FSR role                                                                                                                | Notes                                                                                       |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Planner says “schedule all visits”** (no manual shift creation) | Demand curve → shift assignments; today we create candidate shifts and ESS assigns. With [Timefold: demand-curve shift creation](TIMEFOLD_ROADMAP_RELEVANCE.md#1-demand-curve-based-shift-creation), ESS would create shifts from demand + employees/contracts. | Not used in pre-plan staffing; used after ESS to route visits to assigned employees.                                    | Core dual-model value: one goal (100% coverage), CAIRE handles staffing + routes.           |
| **Demand-driven staffing** (visits → demand curve → staffing)     | Consumes demand curve (hourly demand per day/area); assigns employees to shifts to meet demand. Optional/mandatory shift priorities align with movable vs daily visits.                                                                                         | —                                                                                                                       | Demand curve comes from our pipeline (visits + travel overhead); ESS fills shifts to match. |
| **Caire-Slinga** (AI weekly patterns for movable visits)          | ESS can run per day or over a window; shift patterns feed “who works which days.” Pre-planning may use FSR-first or a lighter pass to establish patterns, then ESS for staffing consistency.                                                                    | FSR optimizes placement of movable visits into day/time slots (pre-planning) and/or routes once ESS has fixed staffing. | Depends on pre-planning flow: FSR for visit placement, ESS for who works when.              |
| **Movable vs fixed visits** (mandatory first, optional fill)      | Mandatory/optional shifts; priority weights (1=mandatory, 5/8=optional). ESS staffs mandatory demand first, then optional.                                                                                                                                      | Mandatory vs optional visits; FSR assigns mandatory first, may leave optional unassigned if capacity is short.          | Priorities kept in sync: same priority scale in both models.                                |

| CAIRE feature                          | ESS role                                                                                                                        | FSR role                                                                                           | Notes                                                                                          |

## Roadmap summary: CAIRE → ESS/FSR

- **Pre-planning / “schedule all visits”:** ESS (and one day possibly [demand-curve shift creation](TIMEFOLD_ROADMAP_RELEVANCE.md#1-demand-curve-based-shift-creation)) for staffing; FSR for visit placement and/or routing once staffing is set.
- **Daily operations:** ESS for who works when; FSR for who visits whom and in what order; both for 0 unassigned, pinning, and optional/mandatory priorities.
- **Continuity:** ESS preferred employees + FSR preferred vehicles and pool; same skill IDs in both.
- **Cost and labor:** ESS for contracts, activation, labor law; FSR for travel; combined metrics.
- **Travel and locations:** ESS for home→area; FSR for routes and maps.
- **Disruption:** ESS + FSR recommendations and real-time re-plan; what-if via [Timefold Copilot](TIMEFOLD_ROADMAP_RELEVANCE.md#3-what-if-scenarios-eg-via-copilot) when available.
- **Comparison and tuning:** ESS+FSR solutions vs baseline; target efficiency and [hyper-tuning](TIMEFOLD_ROADMAP_RELEVANCE.md#4-automated-hyper-tuning-of-constraint-weights--setting-targets-for-metrics); [unassigned visualization](TIMEFOLD_ROADMAP_RELEVANCE.md#2-overview-of-unassigned-shifts-in-the-visualization) for debugging.

Use this roadmap when prioritizing CAIRE features, designing the ESS/FSR pipeline, or aligning with the Timefold roadmap.
